Chappell Roan.

Chappell Roan

AKA: Kayleigh Rose Amstutz
POP INDIE POP ELECTROPOP 2015-Present

Chappell Roan, born Kayleigh Rose Amstutz on February 19, 1998, in the small town of Willard, Missouri, is a rapidly rising artist who has carved out a unique space in the world of pop music. Her upbringing in the Midwest shaped her down-to-earth personality and provided a backdrop of quiet inspiration. As a child, she immersed herself in music, singing in church choirs and writing poetry, which later translated into songwriting. The decision to adopt the stage name Chappell Roan was a tribute to her grandfather and a reflection of her desire to honor her family roots while embracing a larger-than-life persona.

Roan’s career took off when she signed with Atlantic Records as a teenager, releasing her first EP, “School Nights” (2017), which included the breakout single “Good Hurt.” The EP showcased her soulful vocals and emotional depth, drawing early comparisons to indie pop artists like Lana Del Rey. However, her journey was not without challenges; after parting ways with the label, Chappell faced a pivotal moment in her career. Determined to stay true to her artistic vision, she turned to self-releasing her music, gaining a dedicated following for her honest and bold storytelling.

In 2020, she unveiled “Pink Pony Club,” a glittering anthem celebrating queer joy and self-discovery. The song marked a shift in her style, embracing theatricality and vibrant pop melodies while highlighting themes of acceptance and identity.
