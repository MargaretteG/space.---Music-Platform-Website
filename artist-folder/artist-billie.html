Billie Eilish

AKA: Billie Eilish O'Connel
POP ELECTROPOP ALTERNATIVE POP 2015-Present

Billie Eilish Pirate Baird O’Connell, born on December 18, 2001, in Los Angeles, California, is one of the most influential and groundbreaking artists of her generation. Raised in a family of musicians and actors, Billie grew up immersed in creativity. She began writing songs with her brother, Finneas O’Connell, at a young age, crafting tracks that blended her introspective lyrics with Finneas’ innovative production techniques. At just 14 years old, Billie gained global recognition with her viral single “Ocean Eyes,” which was initially written for a dance performance.

In 2019, Billie released her debut album, “When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go?” The album, featuring hits like “bad guy” and “bury a friend,” became a cultural phenomenon, earning her five Grammy Awards, including Album of the Year. Billie’s music is defined by its genre-defying sound, ranging from minimalist electropop to soulful ballads, and her lyrics often explore themes of mental health, fame, and the complexities of adolescence.

Beyond music, Billie has become a fashion icon, known for her bold, oversized outfits and commitment to sustainable design. She is also an advocate for climate change awareness, using her platform to inspire action among her fans.
